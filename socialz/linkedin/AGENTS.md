# Social Media Conversion Guide

## LinkedIn

### Format Rules
- First 2 lines are the hook — this is all that shows before "see more." Make them count.
- Short paragraphs: 1-3 sentences max. Lots of whitespace between paragraphs.
- Total length: 1,500-2,500 words. Long enough to be substantive, short enough to not lose people.
- Use numbered lists and arrow (→) bullets for scannability.
- No markdown links — LinkedIn doesn't render them.
- No hashtags.
- No mentions of links, comments, or "link in comments." The post should stand on its own.

### Tone
- Conversational, direct. Write like you're explaining something to a smart friend.
- Match the tone of the original blog post — don't make it more corporate or "LinkedIn-brained."
- No engagement bait ("Agree?", "Thoughts?", "Most people don't know this...").
- No emojis.

### Accuracy
- Stay true to the original post. Don't overstate claims or sound more confident than the source material. If the blog hedges, the LinkedIn version should hedge too.
- Keep terminology and facts consistent with the blog post.

### Content Strategy
- Lead with the most surprising or counterintuitive claim from the post.
- Cut all sourcing/links — the blog post has them. The LinkedIn version is the teaser.
- Keep the core argument structure but compress heavily. If the blog post has 8 sections, the LinkedIn version should cover 3-4 key points.
- Include specific numbers and data points — they stop the scroll.
- Prioritize counterintuitive insights that resonate with executives (e.g., cost paradoxes, spending trends) over purely technical details.
- End with the broader takeaway, not a summary of the post.

### File Naming
- Mirror the blog post filename: `YYYY-MM-DD-slug.md`
- Save in `socialz/linkedin/`
